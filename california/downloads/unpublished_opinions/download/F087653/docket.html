11/19/2024 Order filed. The court is in receipt of a letter from appellant, filed on November 14, 2024, in which appellant requests permission to file a supplemental brief. Appellant is represented by appointed counsel. "Once appointed, the attorney has the exclusive right to appear and control court proceedings as long as fundamental rights are not denied; neither the party himself nor another attorney can be recognized in the conduct or disposition of the case." (ln re Walker (1976) 56 Cal.App.3d 225, 228; People v. Scott (1998) 64 Cal.App.4th 550.) Consequently, appellant's request is denied.

The Clerk/Executive Officer of this court is directed to forward a copy of appellant's letter to appointed counsel for his consideration.

Appellant's opening brief remains due for filing on or before December 5, 2024.

01/28/2026 Order filed. It has come to this court's attention that juror names appear in volume 26, pages 1987, 1988, and 2031 of the reporter's transcript in violation of Code of Civil Procedure section 237 and California Rules of Court, rule 8.332.

Therefore, within 15 days of the date of this order, the Clerk of the Superior Court of Kern County is hereby directed to: (1) redact the juror's name on pages 1987, 1988, and 2031 of the reporter's transcript; (2) transmit a paper copy of the redacted pages to any counsel who received the record in paper; and (3) electronically transmit an entire redacted reporter's transcript to this court and any party who received the record electronically.

Counsel for any party who received the record electronically is directed to (1) replace the original reporter's transcript with the redacted transcript; (2) destroy all
notes made regarding the jurors' names; and (3) not disclose the jurors' names to any person, agency, group or other entity.

Counsel for any party who received the record in paper is hereby directed to:

(1) remove the unredacted original of the aforementioned pages from the reporter's transcript; (2) reinsert the redacted version in place of the original; (3) send the original and any copies to this court within 10 days of the date of this order; (4) destroy all notes made regarding the jurors' names; and (5) not disclose the jurors' names to any person, agency, group or other entity.

The Clerk/Executive Officer of this court is directed to (1) replace the original reporter's transcript with the redacted transcript; and (2) seal the unredacted originals and treat them as being confidential.
