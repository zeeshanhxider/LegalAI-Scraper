01/12/2026 Order filed. THE COURT:
Respondent superior court is ordered to show cause before this court why the petition for extraordinary writ should not be granted. A decision in this case will determine the merits of the petition. (Welf. & Inst. Code, § 366.26, subd. (l); Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.452(h).) Any brief in opposition to the petition shall be served and filed on or before January 22, 2026. The opposition shall be deemed the return to this order to show cause. No additional briefs shall be filed without further order of this court. The justices will have fully considered the record and the briefs and do not require oral argument. If any party should wish to request oral argument, the request must be served and filed on or before January 22, 2026, at 12:00 p.m. noon. Pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule 8.452(g), the matter will then be set for oral argument on February 10, 2026 before this court at its courtroom at 350 McAllister Street, San Francisco,
California, or by videoconference.

If not requested by January 22, 2026, at 12:00 p.m. noon, oral argument will be deemed waived. The court will immediately submit the cause for decision and promptly issue its opinion.
01/22/2026 Note: No request for OA received by 1/22/26 12pm deadline
01/22/2026 Opposition filed. OPPOSITION TO PETITION FOR EXTRAORDINARY WRIT AND REQUEST FOR STAY
02/05/2026 Opinion filed. (Signed Unpublished) The petition for extraordinary writ is denied on the merits. (§ 366.26, subd. (l)(1)(c); Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.452.) The request for a stay is denied. Our decision is final as to this court immediately. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.490(b)(2)(A).)
