06/30/2025 Motion filed. Motion for Leave to File Late Preliminary Opposition to Petition of Writ of Mandate or Other Appropriate Relief; Declaration of Priscilla Parker; Declaration of Ognian Gavrilov; Declaration of Brian Harrison; Declaration of Edi Adeyanju; Declaration of Eliezer M. Cohen
07/01/2025 Order filed. THE COURT:
On June 5, 2025, this court filed and served an order requesting preliminary opposition on or before June 26, 2025. The order was served on four attorneys designated as counsel for real parties in interest. No preliminary opposition was filed by the deadline set by this court.

On June 30, 2025, counsel for real parties in interest filed a "Motion for Leave to File a Late Preliminary Opposition" accompanied by declarations of counsel from all four attorneys that were served with this court's order on June 5, 2025. The motion seeks relief based on "the inadvertent failure to review the notice from the court" and the alleged "non-receipt of notice from the court."

The court does not accept that notice of its order was not received. Two attorneys, Priscilla Parker and Edi Adeyanju, filed declarations admitting the notice was received but not read (in one case because counsel was busy with a jury trial and in one case because the notification went to a "Spam" folder). Two attorneys, Brian Harrison and Ognian Gavrilov, filed declarations stating "it appears" the TrueFiling Service Notifications were not received because they were unable to locate the Notification in their email inboxes. The court observes that no attorney attests to an error in the email addresses.

Counsel for real parties in interest are strongly admonished to ensure they timely review and comply with all orders of this court in the future.

The motion requests an additional ten (10) days to file the preliminary opposition. Reluctantly, the court grants the request for an additional ten days to file a preliminary opposition. The preliminary opposition is due on or before July 11, 2025. No further extensions of time for the preliminary opposition are contemplated.

Petitioner may file and serve a reply within fourteen (14) days after the preliminary opposition is served and filed.
