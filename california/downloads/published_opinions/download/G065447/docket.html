05/16/2025 Order on motion filed. Petitioner's motion to file portions of the petition for writ of mandate
and exhibits under seal is GRANTED.

The court finds that because the unredacted petition and exhibits
contain references to confidential medical records, there exists an overriding
interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record, the overriding
interest supports sealing the record, a substantial probability exists that the
overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed, the proposed
sealing is narrowly tailored, and no less restrictive means exists to achieve
the overriding interest under these facts. (Cal. Rules of Court, rules 8.46,
2.550.)

The clerk of this court is directed to file forthwith under seal the
unredacted petition for writ of mandate and the 2 volumes of unredacted
exhibits received by this court on April 25, 2025. The clerk is further directed
to maintain the sealed record in a secure location separate from the public
record.

The clerk of this court is also directed to redact the addresses and the
telephone numbers of victims and witnesses on pages 9 through 12, 15, 17,
19, 21, 37 through 39, 43, 45, 47 and 49 in volume 2 of the public version of
the redacted exhibits, and petitioner's driver's license number on page 156 in
volume 1, and on pages 11, 15, 43, 66, and 83 of volume 2 of the public
version of the redacted exhibits.

It is further ordered that counsel refile the public and redacted version
of the petition for writ of mandate no later than May 23, 2025, without
images embedded at pages 7 and 8 of the petition. (Cal. Rules of Court, rules
8.485(a), 8.204.)

Real party in interest is ordered to file opposition to the petition no
later than June 5, 2025.
O'Leary, P.J.

11/12/2025 Order filed. This case is set for oral argument on November 19, 2025, at 1:30
p.m. Counsel is requested to focus on the following issues:
1. Whether the trial court applied the wrong legal standard and,
therefore, abused its discretion, when it concluded Siam would not respond
to treatment, notwithstanding Dr. Grajewski's opinion, and the lack of any
expert opinion to the contrary or challenge to her credentials. (See Pen. Code
§ 1001.36, subd. (c)(1).)
2. Whether the trial court's finding of an unreasonable risk of
danger to public safety is supported by substantial evidence.
3. If this court were to reverse the trial court's decision, what
disposition and/or instructions to the trial court would be appropriate?
