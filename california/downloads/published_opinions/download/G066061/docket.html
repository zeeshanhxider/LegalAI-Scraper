N.D. v. The Superior Court of Orange County
Case Number G066061
Date Description Notes
10/07/2025 Filed petition for writ of: Petitioner Nicole Dodson, Writ of mandate
10/07/2025 Application for waiver of filing fee filed. - received only -
10/17/2025 Order filed. THE COURT:*
The petition for writ of mandate is DENIED.
On the court's own motion, pursuant to California Rules of Court,
rule 8.492, Attorney T. Matthew Phillips and petitioner N.D. are ordered to
show cause why this court should not impose sanctions, including reporting
T. Matthew Phillips to the California State Bar, on the grounds: (1) this
petition was frivolous (Cal. Rules of Court, rules 8.492(a)(1) & 8.486(b)(1),
Rules Prof. Conduct, rule 8.2(a), Bus. & Prof. Code, § 6068, subd. (b)); and (2)
the factual contentions made therein had no evidentiary support (Cal. Rules
of Court, rules 8.492(a)(2), 8.486(b)(1), & 8.204(a)(1)(C)).This order shall constitute notice to Attorney T. Matthew Phillips
and petitioner, N.D., that pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule 8.492,
and this court's inherent supervisory powers, the court is considering the
imposition of sanctions that may be payable to real party in interest and/or
payable to this court.
Attorney T. Matthew Phillips and petitioner N.D. may submit,
within 10 days of this order, an opposition to the court's order to show cause.
(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.492(b).)
Real party in interest, E.F., may simultaneously submit, within
10 days of this order, a response commenting upon the court's proposed
action, as well as the type and amount of sanctions if this court were to
impose sanctions.
Pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule 8.492(d), any request
for oral argument on the court's order to show cause must be filed within 10
days of this order. (Ibid.) If a request for oral argument as to the court's order
to show cause is filed by either party, the clerk of this court is DIRECTED to
place the order to show cause on the November 2025 oral argument calendar.
Any request for oral argument filed by either party must include that party's
availability for oral argument during the week of November 17, 2025. If no
request for oral argument is filed within 10 days of this order, the clerk of
this court is DIRECTED to submit the cause on the court's order to show
cause for decision.
Attorney T. Matthew Phillips and petitioner N.D., as well as real
party in interest E.F., are invited and encouraged to address, in addition to
and in connection with the above, the following issue:
Whether the following statements asserted in the petition have
evidentiary support in the record which was provided to this court inconjunction with the petition and, if not, whether their assertion(s)
warrant(s) sanctions:
" The trial court's courtroom "actively retaliates" against
petitioner (see petition, p. 10);
" The trial court "systematically discriminates" against
petitioner (see petition p. 5);
" The trial court "acts in concert with [petitioner's]
opponents" (see petition, p. 5);
" The trial court "openly and notoriously champion[s] the
cause of Petitioner's opponents" (see petition, p. 3);
" The trial court "purposely failed to serve [a DVRO] on
[petitioner]" "because if [petitioner] were served with the
temporary DVRO" it would "tip-off' petitioner "which ruins
the element of surprise when the opponents send a process
server to personally serve [petitioner] with the DVRO" (see
petition, p. 6);
" Certain court orders are "fake" (see petition p. 12);
" Certain court orders are "forged" (see petition, p. 12);
" A court hearing "magically disappear[ed]" "because Her
Honor ha[d] no wish to explain whether (or not) she penned
the now-infamous [forged] judicial signature . . . . " (see
petition, p. 12).

DELANEY, ACTING P. J.

rule or order changing time available for argument], the court ORDERS the

Each side is allowed up to 15 minutes for oral argument. Where there are
more than two parties, a side consists of all parties whose interests are not adverse.
If there are more than two parties represented by separate counsel who request oral
argument, or if counsel for amicus curiae requests oral argument, the court may
apportion or expand the time according to the respective interests of the parties and
of amicus curiae.
Any request for additional time for oral argument must be made by written
application submitted to the court within 10 calendar days of the date of the order
setting oral argument. The application must be served contemporaneously on all
other parties and must specify the amount of time requested and the issues to
which additional oral argument will be addressed. When an application is granted,
the time allotted to the other side or sides will be similarly enlarged.
